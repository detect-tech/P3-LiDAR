MUST-ASK (highest priority — ask these first, ~10-15 min)

1) Can you actually share a sample KML/route file with us today, even a
   redacted or non-sensitive one? Transmission structure locations can be
   sensitive/critical infrastructure — if real data is off the table, we need
   to know NOW so we build against synthetic/public data instead of stalling
   mid-hackathon waiting on approval.

2) Walk us through what happens today, step by step, when your team needs to
   decide "do we fly new LiDAR, or is there something we can reuse?" Who makes
   that call, and what do they look at to make it? (This is the actual
   workflow we're replacing — the demo should map onto it directly.)

3) What does a "win" look like to you, specifically? If you saw a demo at 3PM
   that made you say "we should build this," what would be in it? (Ask this
   explicitly — it's the difference between a cute map and something that
   reads as business value to leadership.)

4) What coordinate system / format is your structure or route data actually
   in? (GPS lat/lon, State Plane, shapefile, KML, something else) — we've
   already hit this ourselves: LiDAR tiles are commonly in State Plane feet,
   not lat/lon, and silently assuming WGS84 is a real integration bug, not a
   nitpick.

5) Roughly what scale are we talking about — one proposed route, a handful,
   or your whole transmission network? This changes whether "map view" needs
   to be a lightweight client-side tool (what we've prototyped) or something
   built for a much bigger dataset.

IMPORTANT (ask if time allows, ~10 min)

6) How often does new LiDAR get flown or new route data show up — is this a
   one-time catalog or does it need to stay current? (Affects whether the
   "coverage catalog" is a snapshot or a living system — a bigger scope claim
   we shouldn't make casually in the demo.)

7) What do you currently use for GIS / mapping internally (ArcGIS, something
   in-house, etc.)? Not to integrate today, but so our demo doesn't propose
   something that ignores an existing system they already trust.

8) Roughly, what does a duplicate/unnecessary LiDAR collection cost you today
   — in dollars, in schedule, in both? Even a rough number turns "reduces
   duplicate collection" into a business case with real weight behind it
   ($ and weeks saved) instead of a vague benefit.

9) Who's the actual audience for this tool's output day-to-day — the same
   people planning routes, or does it get handed up to
   compliance/finance/leadership? Changes whether "stats/viz" should look like
   an internal working view or something presentation-ready.

10) What do you need FROM us — as an output — for this to be useful to you
    tomorrow, not just today? (e.g. a filtered/uncovered KML export, a shareable
    report, something else). Ask this one directly, don't assume the KML
    export idea is what they actually want.

NICE TO HAVE (only if the conversation naturally goes there)

11) Are there other data sources beyond LiDAR you'd want cataloged the same
    way (survey data, as-built drawings, imagery)?
12) Is there a specific real (or realistic) study area/route you'd want us to
    demo against, so the 3PM presentation lands as "our actual problem," not
    a generic Miami-Dade example?
